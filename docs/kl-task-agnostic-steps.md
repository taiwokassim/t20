### Purpose

This playbook defines **Task-Agnostic Steps (TAS)** as universal, reusable cognitive primitives for KickLang workflows. TAS enable any agent, module, or system to decompose goals, coordinate actions, and transfer knowledge independently of domain or objective[web:9].

---

### When to Use

- Decomposing complex goals into atomic, trans-domain steps.
- Structuring modular workflows for cross-agent collaboration.
- Aligning multi-role pipelines with meta-cognitive coherence.
- Iteratively refining and purifying procedural archetypes for reuse.

---

### Step-by-Step Workflow

1. **Identify/Extract**
   - Break high-level goals into general, trans-domain steps.
   - Map informal intent â†’ explicit, reusable TAS units.

2. **Structure Workflows**
   - Sequence TAS units into coherent operational flows.
   - Encode dependencies, ordering, and branching logic.

3. **Layer Cognition**
   - Align actions and goals across temporal and cognitive frames.
   - Enable nested plans, recursive reasoning, and cognitive zooming.

4. **Transfer Knowledge**
   - Synthesize information and communicate transformations between TAS.
   - Allow inter-role learning and procedural reflection.

5. **Provide Context**
   - Situate each TAS in its operational environment.
   - Bind local actions to their global system or environmental state.

6. **Purify TAS**
   - Abstract, simplify, and refine the TAS set for universality.
   - Evolve through feedback-driven refinement.
